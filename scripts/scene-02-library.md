# Scene 2: The False Friend "Library"

**Setting:** A small French bookshop (librairie). A tourist (English speaker) enters.

**Characters:**
- **Tourist:** English speaker, learning French
- **Shopkeeper:** French bookshop owner

---

**Dialogue:**

**Tourist:** (looking around) Bonjour! Est-ce que je peux emprunter des livres ici?  
*(Hello! Can I borrow books here?)*

**Shopkeeper:** (confused) Emprunter? Mais... c'est une librairie, pas une bibliothèque!  
*(Borrow? But... this is a bookshop, not a library!)*

**Tourist:** Ah! Je suis désolé. En anglais, "library" est un endroit où on emprunte des livres.  
*(Ah! I'm sorry. In English, "library" is a place where you borrow books.)*

**Shopkeeper:** (smiling) Ah, les faux amis! Ici, "librairie" veut dire "bookshop". Vous voulez acheter un livre?  
*(Ah, false friends! Here, "librairie" means "bookshop". Do you want to buy a book?)*

**Tourist:** Oui, je vais acheter un livre. Merci de m'avoir corrigé!  
*(Yes, I'll buy a book. Thank you for correcting me!)*

**Shopkeeper:** Pas de problème! Et n'oubliez pas: "bibliothèque" = library, "librairie" = bookshop. Facile, non?  
*(No problem! And don't forget: "bibliothèque" = library, "librairie" = bookshop. Easy, right?)*

**Tourist:** Très facile! Merci encore!  
*(Very easy! Thank you again!)*

---

**Learning point:** The false friend "library" (English) vs "librairie" (French). "Librairie" means bookshop, while "bibliothèque" means library.
